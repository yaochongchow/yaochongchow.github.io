How I Work

Clear systems thinking is part of the product.

I work best on systems where the hard part is not just shipping code, but understanding behavior under load, narrowing failures quickly, and making the architecture easy to reason about for the next engineer.

Measure before optimizing

I trust latency distributions, throughput numbers, packet loss, and incident patterns more than intuition. The best engineering decisions usually start with visibility.

Instrument before guessing

Observability is not cleanup work after the fact. I want the traces, logs, metrics, and replay paths that make debugging faster when the system stops behaving the way it should.

Design for failure modes

I care about what happens when requests race, devices drop packets, services drift out of sync, or state becomes stale. That is usually where systems engineering stops being generic.

Keep systems explainable

The architecture should be simple enough to defend in an interview and clear enough that a teammate can debug it without folklore. Complexity only earns its place when it solves a real constraint.
