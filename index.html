About

I build systems that have to work when things start going wrong.

I’m most interested in problems where behavior isn’t clean. Requests race, devices drop packets, telemetry arrives out of order, and systems degrade under load. That’s where architecture, observability, and tradeoffs stop being theoretical and start mattering.

I’ve worked across embedded firmware, backend services, and real-time pipelines, focusing on making systems not just functional, but reliable and explainable when conditions aren’t ideal.

What I build

Backend services, real-time data pipelines, and distributed systems that connect APIs, infrastructure, and real-world inputs.

How I work

  • Measure before optimizing
  • Instrument before guessing
  • Design for failure, not just the happy path
  • Keep systems debuggable under pressure

What I pay attention to

Latency distributions, packet loss, retries, stale state, queueing behavior, and the decisions that determine whether a system is easy or painful to debug later.
