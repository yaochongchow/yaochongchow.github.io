Approach

I build across APIs, infrastructure, and real-world constraints.

The thread through this portfolio is not a list of technologies. It is a way of working: measure the system, instrument the edges, understand the failure modes, and keep the architecture legible enough that other engineers can reason about it.

Cross-layer ownership

I am comfortable moving between API design, database performance, telemetry pipelines, firmware-adjacent debugging, dashboards, and cloud deployment. That matters when the bottleneck is not isolated to one layer.

What I look for

I tend to care most about behavior that can be measured and explained: latency, throughput, packet loss, MTTR, queueing, retries, and the small design decisions that make systems reliable or painful.

How I think

  • Measure before optimizing.
  • Instrument before guessing.
  • Design around failure modes, not just happy paths.
  • Keep systems explainable enough to debug under pressure.
