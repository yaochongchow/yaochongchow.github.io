About

I build systems that have to behave correctly under real constraints.

The common thread in my work is not a stack. It is system behavior. I like problems where requests race, devices drop packets, telemetry arrives out of order, or a backend starts failing at the tail. That is where observability, tradeoffs, and architecture stop being abstract and start mattering.

What I build

I build backend services, telemetry pipelines, cloud-native systems, and robotics-adjacent software. The best fit for me is work where APIs, data flow, infrastructure, and real-world constraints all interact.

What I’m looking for

I am looking for backend, distributed systems, infrastructure, platform, robotics software, and real-time systems roles where reliability, performance, and ownership matter more than buzzwords.

How I usually work

  • Measure before optimizing.
  • Instrument before guessing.
  • Design around failure modes, not just happy paths.
  • Keep systems explainable enough to debug under pressure.

What I pay attention to

I pay attention to the parts that make systems feel real: latency distributions, packet loss, retries, stale state, queueing, MTTR, and the design decisions that make debugging easier or harder later.

Contact

Open to difficult systems problems with real operating constraints.
