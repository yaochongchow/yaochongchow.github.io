About

Backend systems that stay predictable in production.

I build backend systems that have to stay reliable when things start going wrong. I care about latency, failure modes, observability, and the debugging work required to make systems trustworthy in production.

I work across device telemetry, backend services, and cloud infrastructure, which helps me reason about the full path from data generation to system behavior. Embedded experience improves how I design backend systems under real-world constraints, while backend and real-time systems remain my core focus.

What I build

Backend APIs, telemetry ingestion pipelines, and distributed services with clear boundaries and predictable production behavior.

How I work

  • Design for concurrency, failure, and recovery
  • Instrument before tuning or scaling
  • Use metrics and traces to isolate bottlenecks
  • Keep incident debugging fast and repeatable

What I pay attention to

Tail latency, retries, stale state, queueing, data ordering, and the architectural choices that determine whether a system is reliable and debuggable at scale.
